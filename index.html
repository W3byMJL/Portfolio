Background

I've known Graham since 2014, and have known of his background in breakthrough energy research. Visting his home lab I was able to see some of his work, as well as learning about presentations at conferences. After we both departed that previous job, he and I remained friends and I would see some of his social media posts.After I dove into learning web development, I reached out and asked if I could build a web presence for his invention. We had good talks about how to showcase the research and the invention without giving away "the secret sauce". Below you will find 3 iterations of a website I built for him.

Lessons Learned

  • People don't always know what they want in terms of layout, colors etc. Very often they have a vision but don't know how to articulate this into clear marching orders. You have to show them options.
  • The faster you fail at prototyping a product, the faster you can come up with a product that you like. I tried to use this approach with using my own imagination, pre-built templates, and artificial intelligence to come up with mock ups to help stear a direction.

First Round

I remember Graham would get circuit boards made, and they were typically a purple - so I started with a purple and yellow theme to capture the this vision of circuit boards. I wanted a one page layout with a top navigation menu that would let you click and jump to the various sections with "id" attribute. I visited his lab, took a few pictures and created a quick mock up site to showcase some of his inventions and as a way to get people interested and hopefully donate to the research. color.

Second Round

After the first version was out and to him for review I wanted to contrast that with a few other layout options. One was a more classic black and white "blog" style utilizing a prebuild template. The second style was developed consulting Chat GPT. First I wanted some brand ideas for a color scheme that would be suitable for a renewable/breakthrough energy company. Chat GPT delivered a few suggestions and why those colors represent something I should choose. I also asked for help with a simple HTML/CSS layout as well as code for some other things like a bio section and donation button, and I used Chat GPT to help craft some of wording for the various content I wanted to include.
